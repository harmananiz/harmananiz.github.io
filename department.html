University of Toronto: The Institute of Communication, Culture, Information and Technology Website


Project Overview

During our user bservation of the department website, we identified several concerns that impact the user experience. One prominent issue is an overwhelming interface, characterized by an excessive amount of information and an unclear information architecture. This abundance of content creates a cognitive burden for users, making it challenging for them to navigate and find the information they need.
Another significant concern is the presence of three different navigation tabs on the website, each serving a distinct purpose. This multiple-tab approach leads to confusion and makes it difficult for users to locate the relevant information they are seeking. A unified and simplified navigation system would greatly enhance the user experience and improve usability.
Additionally, the ICCIT website suffers from information overload due to redundant elements such as the carousel and an excessive number of links. These elements contribute to the overall clutter and make it harder for users to focus on the essential information. Removing or reorganizing these elements would help streamline the website and reduce cognitive load.
Furthermore, there is a lack of prioritization for first-year students, with non-relevant information and links taking up valuable space. The website should be tailored to meet the needs of this specific user group, providing easy access to essential resources and relevant information related to the ICCIT program.
The use of symbols that are not universally recognized poses another challenge, potentially creating cultural constraints for certain international students. It is crucial to ensure that visual elements and symbols used on the website are globally understood to foster inclusivity and improve communication.

In light of these concerns, it is recommended to simplify the interface, reorganize the information architecture, and prioritize content for first-year students. By addressing these issues, the ICCIT website can provide a more intuitive and user-friendly experience.
